Matrix Operations

\(\def\M{\mathrm{M}}\) Let \(K\) be an associative ring and let \(A=(a_{ij}),\; B=(b_{ij})\in \M_{m,n}(K)\). Then the sum of the matrices \(A\) and \(B\) is, by definition, \[A+B = (a_{ij}+b_{ij}).\] Clearly, \(A+B\in\M_{m,n}(K)\) and addition of matrices is associative and commutative. The null matrix in \(\M_{m,n}(K)\) is the matrix 0, all entries of which are zero. For every \(A\in\M_{m,n}(K)\), \[A+0=0+A=A\]
